The **Croissant** is the ultimate emblem of the French breakfast. Did you know it originates from the Austrian _Kipferl_? Reworked by Parisian bakers in the early 20th century, it has become this masterpiece of "Leavened Puff Pastry" (Pâte Feuilletée Levée), where the lightness of brioche meets the crispness of puff pastry.

---

## 🛠️ Technical Sheet

- **Preparation time:** 1.5 hours (spread over 2 days)
- **Resting time (Cold):** 12h to 24h
- **Proofing time:** 2h to 2.30h
- **Cooking time:** 15-20 minutes
- **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Expert)
- **Servings:** 12 to 15 croissants

## 🛒 Ingredients

- **500g** T45 strong flour (or a mix of T45/T55)
- **60g** granulated sugar
- **10g** sea salt
- **20g** fresh baker's yeast
- **100ml** cold water
- **150ml** cold whole milk
- **50g** soft butter (for the dough)
- **250g** dry butter / tourage butter (minimum 82% fat)
- **1 egg** for glazing

---

## 👨‍🍳 Detailed Method (Step by Step)

### Step 1: The Dough (Day 1)

Mix the flour, sugar, salt, and soft butter. Dissolve the yeast in the cold water/milk mixture and pour over the flour. Knead in a mixer (slow speed) for 5 to 7 minutes. The dough should be firm and homogeneous. Form a ball, score a cross, and let rest for **30 min** in the fridge, then flatten into a rectangle and wrap in cling film. Leave overnight in the refrigerator.

### Step 2: Layering (The Secret of the Flakiness)

Roll out your cold tourage butter between two sheets of parchment paper to form a square. Take out the cold dough and roll it into a rectangle twice as large as the butter. Place the butter in the center and fold the dough to enclose it completely.

- **Single Fold:** Roll the dough into a long strip, fold it in three (like a letter). Turn by 90°. Let rest for 30 min in the cold.
- **Double Fold:** Roll out again, fold both ends toward the center, then fold in half. Let rest for 1h in the cold.

### Step 3: Shaping

Roll out the dough to 3-4mm thickness to obtain a large rectangle. Cut out isosceles triangles (base of 9-10 cm, height of 25 cm). Lightly stretch the tip of the triangle. Roll them from the base toward the tip without tightening too much.

### Step 4: Final Rise (Proofing)

Place the croissants on a baking sheet. Let them rise for **2h to 2.5h** in a warm place (maximum 26-28°C so as not to melt the butter). They should double in volume and "tremble" slightly when the plate is moved.

### Step 5: Baking

Preheat the oven to **190°C** (fan-assisted). Gently glaze the croissants with egg using a brush (avoid putting glaze on the visible edge layers). Bake for **15 to 20 minutes**.

---

## 💡 Chef's Tips

- **Temperature:** This is the key factor. Always work in a kitchen that is not too hot and on a cold work surface. If the dough softens, put it back in the cold immediately.
- **Butter:** Use a so-called "dry" or "tourage" butter. Standard supermarket butter will contain too much water and break the layers.
- **Cooling:** Let cool on a rack to keep the crispness underneath.
