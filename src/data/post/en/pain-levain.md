**Sourdough Bread** (Pain au Levain) is the ancestor of all breads. Unlike yeast-leavened bread, it uses a natural fermentation from wild microorganisms present in the flour and air. The result: exceptional longevity, a tangy crumb, and much better digestibility.

---

## 🛠️ Technical Sheet

- **Preparation time:** 40 minutes (spread out)
- **Fermentation time:** 12h to 24h
- **Cooking time:** 45 minutes
- **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Patience required)
- **Servings:** 1 large 800g loaf

## 🛒 Ingredients

- **500g** strong flour (T65, T80, or a mix with Rye)
- **325ml** non-chlorinated water (spring or filtered)
- **100g** active natural sourdough starter (bubbly and ready to use)
- **10g** sea salt

---

## 👨‍🍳 Detailed Method (Step by Step)

### Step 1: Mixing and Autolyse

Mix the flour and water. Let rest for 1 hour. This step allows the enzymes to begin their work and facilitates the development of the gluten network without intensive kneading.

### Step 2: Incorporating Starter and Salt

Add your active starter and salt. Knead lightly by hand (pinch method) to mix well. The dough will be sticky; this is normal.

### Step 3: Stretch & Fold

Instead of a classic kneading, we will give the dough strength through stretching. For **3 hours**, every 30 minutes, grab one side of the dough, stretch it upward, and fold it over the center. Do this for all 4 sides.

### Step 4: Bulk Fermentation

Let the dough rest at room temperature until it increases in volume by about 50% and shows bubbles on the surface.

### Step 5: Shaping and Cold Retard

Shape it into a ball (boule) or an elongated shape (bâtard) by creating tension on the surface of the dough. Place it in a floured "banneton" (proofing basket), seam side up. Place it in the refrigerator for **12h to 18h**. This slow fermentation develops the flavors and makes scoring easier.

### Step 6: Baking (Ideally in a Dutch Oven)

Preheat your oven (and the empty Dutch oven) to **250°C**. Turn the bread onto parchment paper. Score deeply. Place it in the hot Dutch oven and close the lid.

- **25 min** with lid on (the bread's own steam allows it to puff up well).
- **20 min** without lid (to color and harden the crust).

---

## 💡 Chef's Tips

- **The Starter:** Test your starter before starting: drop a spoonful into a glass of water; if it floats, it's ready!
- **Water:** Chlorine kills sourdough bacteria. Let tap water sit for 1 hour exposed to the air before use, or use filtered water.
- **Cooling:** Wait at least 2 hours before cutting sourdough bread. This is called "curing": the inner moisture must stabilize, otherwise the crumb will be sticky.
