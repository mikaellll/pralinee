The story of the **Tarte Tatin** is one of a wonderful blunder. At the end of the 19th century, sisters Stéphanie and Caroline Tatin, who ran a hotel-restaurant in Lamotte-Beuvron, reportedly put a tart into the oven upside down by mistake. This cult dessert is now a symbol of French indulgence, combining the melting of candied apples with the crunch of the pastry.

---

## 🛠️ Technical Sheet

- **Preparation time:** 45 minutes
- **Resting time:** 1 hour (for the dough)
- **Cooking time:** 50 minutes
- **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐ (Medium)
- **Servings:** 6 to 8 people

## 🛒 Ingredients

- **8 to 10** Reinette or Golden apples (apples that hold up in cooking)
- **150g** granulated sugar
- **80g** semi-salted butter (the salt enhances the caramel)
- **1** roll of puff or shortcrust pastry (preferably homemade)
- **Optional:** A pinch of cinnamon or vanilla

---

## 👨‍🍳 Detailed Method (Step by Step)

### Step 1: The Dough (Prepare in Advance)

If you make your dough yourself, prepare a shortcrust pastry rich in butter. Let it rest for 1 hour in the cold before rolling it out.

### Step 2: Preparing the Apples

Peel the apples, core them, and cut them into large quarters (into 4). Do not cut them too thinly; they must remain whole and melting after a long bake.

### Step 3: "Dry" Caramel

In a Tatin mold (copper or cast iron mold that goes on the stove), pour the sugar. Heat over medium heat without stirring until you get an amber caramel. Then add the diced semi-salted butter off the heat. Watch out for splashes! Mix to get a smooth caramel cream at the bottom of the mold.

### Step 4: Arrangement

Arrange the apple quarters tightly in the mold, standing on their sides. Don't hesitate to put many in, as apples shrink significantly during cooking. You can add a second layer of apples on top to fill the gaps.

### Step 5: First Cooking

Cook the apples directly in the mold on the stove for **10-15 minutes**. They will start to candid in the caramel and butter.

### Step 6: Baking

Cover the apples with the pastry disc. Tuck the pastry in along the edges of the mold (as if you were tucking in a bed). Bake at **180°C for 35 to 40 minutes**. The pastry should be well-browned and crispy.

### Step 7: Unmolding (The Moment of Truth)

Let rest for 10 minutes out of the oven so the caramel stabilizes slightly. Place a large plate over the mold and flip with a quick and confident gesture. **Warning:** The hot caramel can run; protect your hands!

---

## 💡 Chef's Tips

- **The Apples:** Avoid overly juicy apples that would release water. The French Reinette is the queen of the Tatin.
- **Tasting:** Serve the Tatin tart lukewarm, accompanied by a generous spoonful of thick crème fraîche or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.
- **The Mold:** A good copper Tatin mold allows for perfect heat distribution for a homogeneous caramel.
