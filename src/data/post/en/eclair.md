The **Éclair** owes its name to how quickly it is eaten... it's gone "in a flash" (un éclair)! Created by Marie-Antoine Carême, the "chef of kings," it requires perfect mastery of choux pastry and exemplary regularity in piping. A beautiful éclair is recognized by its smooth top, its generously filled interior, and its shiny glaze.

---

## 🛠️ Technical Sheet

- **Preparation time:** 1h15
- **Cooking time:** 40 minutes
- **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Advanced)
- **Servings:** 10 to 12 éclairs

## 🛒 Ingredients

### For the Choux Pastry

- **125g** water / **125g** milk
- **100g** butter
- **150g** T55 flour
- **4 to 5** eggs
- **A pinch** of salt and sugar

### For the Chocolate Pastry Cream

- **500ml** whole milk
- **4** egg yolks
- **80g** sugar
- **40g** cornstarch
- **150g** dark chocolate (minimum 66%)

### For the Fondant (Glaze)

- **250g** white pastry fondant
- **20g** syrup (sugar + water)
- **15g** cocoa powder or melted chocolate

---

## 👨‍🍳 Detailed Method (Step by Step)

### Step 1: Making the Puffs

Prepare the choux pastry (see the drying method in the Cream Puffs article). Using a star nozzle (PF16), pipe sticks **12 cm long** on a baking sheet. The star nozzle allows the éclair to puff up more regularly without bursting.

### Step 2: Mastered Baking

Preheat to **170°C**. Bake for **40 minutes** without ever opening the door. At the end of baking, slightly open the oven door for 5 minutes (door blocked with a wooden spoon) to let the remaining moisture escape.

### Step 3: Intense Chocolate Cream

Boil the milk. Whisk the yolks with the sugar, add the cornstarch. Pour in the hot milk, stir, and cook for 1 minute after boiling. Then pour the hot cream over the chopped chocolate. Mix until you get a smooth cream. Film in contact and place in the cold.

### Step 4: Filling

Poke three small holes under each cooled éclair (at the ends and in the center). Fill using a piping bag with a plain nozzle until the cream starts to come out. The éclair should feel heavy. Try not to crack the shell.

### Step 5: Glazing (The Pro Touch)

Heat the fondant in a double boiler up to **35-37°C** maximum (it should be flexible but not liquid). If it exceeds 40°C, it will lose its shine as it cools. Add the cocoa and a little syrup to adjust the texture. Dip the top of the éclair in the fondant, smooth the excess with your finger, and let set.

---

## 💡 Chef's Tips

- **The Glaze:** If you don't have fondant, use a ganache (chocolate + cream) for a simpler and less sweet result.
- **The Nozzle:** Using a star/toothed nozzle is the real secret to éclairs that don't deform.
- **The Rest:** Let the filled éclairs rest for at least 2 hours in the refrigerator before serving so that the shell softens slightly and melts in the mouth.
