The **Black Forest Cake**, or _Schwarzwälder Kirschtorte_, is much more than just a chocolate cake. It is a sensory journey into the heart of the mountain range in southwestern Germany. According to legend, its colors reflect the traditional costume of the women of the region: the black of the dress (chocolate), the white of the blouse (whipped cream), and the red of the pom-poms on the hat (cherries).

In Germany, the law protects its recipe: to bear this name, the cake must mandatory contain **Kirschwasser** (cherry brandy) in a perceptible quantity.

---

## 🛠️ Technical Sheet

- **Preparation time:** 1h30
- **Resting time:** 6 hours (minimum)
- **Cooking time:** 30 minutes
- **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Advanced)
- **Servings:** 8 to 10 people

## 🛒 Ingredients

### For the Moist Chocolate Sponge (Génoise)

- **6** whole eggs
- **150g** granulated sugar
- **120g** T45 flour
- **30g** bitter cocoa powder (Van Houten type)
- **50g** melted butter (lukewarm)

### For Filling and Syrup

- **600g** Sour cherries (Morello) in syrup (save the juice!)
- **4 tbsp** Kirschwasser
- **750ml** liquid heavy cream (minimum 35% fat)
- **80g** powdered sugar
- **1 sachet** whipped cream stabilizer (optional)

### For Decoration

- **100g** dark chocolate shavings
- A few reserved whole cherries

---

## 👨‍🍳 Detailed Method (Step by Step)

### Step 1: Chocolate Sponge (Bain-Marie)

In a bowl, whisk the eggs and sugar over a pan of simmering water (Double boiler/Bain-marie). The mixture should double in volume and become very white and fluffy. Remove from heat and gently fold in the sifted flour and cocoa using a spatula. Finish by folding in the melted butter. Bake at **180°C for 25-30 minutes**. Let cool and cut into three equal discs.

### Step 2: Soaking Syrup

Mix the recovered cherry juice (about 150ml) with the Kirsch. If you prefer not to use alcohol, you can boil the mixture to evaporate the ethanol while keeping the cherry flavor.

### Step 3: Royal Whipped Cream (Chantilly)

Whisk the very cold cream into a firm chantilly. When it starts to set, add the powdered sugar gradually. It should be stiff enough to support the weight of the cake.

### Step 4: Assembly (The Structure)

1. **First disc:** Place the base sponge, soak it generously with syrup using a brush. Spread a 1cm thick layer of whipped cream. Distribute half of the cherries by pressing them down slightly.
2. **Second disc:** Place the second disc, soak it, add more whipped cream and the remaining cherries.
3. **Third disc:** Place the last disc (previously soaked on the inside).

### Step 5: Frosting and Finishing

Cover the entire cake with the remaining whipped cream. Smooth it with a spatula for a clean look. Apply chocolate shavings to the sides. Using a piping bag, form rosettes on top and place a cherry on each.

---

## 💡 Chef's Tips

- **The Rest:** It is IMPERATIVE to let the cake rest for at least 6h in the fridge. This allows the syrup to migrate into the sponge and create that unique melting texture.
- **The Chocolate:** To make beautiful homemade shavings, use a peeler on a chocolate bar at room temperature.
- **The Kirsch:** Don't neglect it; it's the soul of the cake!
