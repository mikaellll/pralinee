The **Cream Puff** (Chou à la crème) is a French pastry institution. Its apparent simplicity — puffed pastry and smooth cream — hides a rigorous technique invented by the Italian pastry chef Popelini in the 16th century. The challenge? To obtain a perfectly regular puff, well-hollowed, and that doesn't collapse after baking.

---

## 🛠️ Technical Sheet

- **Preparation time:** 60 minutes
- **Cooking time:** 35-40 minutes
- **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐ (Medium)
- **Servings:** About 20 puffs

## 🛒 Ingredients

### For the Choux Pastry

- **125ml** water
- **125ml** whole milk
- **110g** unsalted butter (diced)
- **140g** T45 or T55 flour
- **4 to 5** whole eggs (depending on consistency)
- **5g** salt
- **5g** sugar

### For the Vanilla Pastry Cream

- **500ml** whole milk
- **1** Bourbon vanilla bean
- **4** egg yolks
- **90g** sugar
- **45g** cornstarch
- **50g** butter (added at the end)

---

## 👨‍🍳 Detailed Method (Step by Step)

### Step 1: Drying the "Panade"

In a saucepan, bring the water, milk, butter, salt, and sugar to a boil. As soon as it boils, remove from heat and pour in the flour all at once. Mix vigorously with a spatula. Return to medium heat for **1 to 2 minutes**, stirring constantly to "dry out" the dough (it should pull away from the sides and form a thin film at the bottom).

### Step 2: Incorporating the Eggs

Transfer the dough to a mixer with the paddle attachment (or a bowl). Let cool for a few moments. Add the eggs one by one, waiting until each egg is perfectly incorporated before adding the next.
**The test:** The dough is ready when it forms a satiny ribbon or a "cock's comb" at the end of the spatula. If you draw a line with your finger, the groove should close very slowly.

### Step 3: Piping

Preheat your oven to **170°C** (static heat preferred). Using a plain piping bag, pipe puffs 3-4 cm in diameter onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Space them well as they will double in volume.

### Step 4: Baking (Never open the oven!)

Bake for **35 to 40 minutes**. **Important:** Never open the oven door during the first 25 minutes, otherwise your puffs might collapse. They are cooked when they are golden brown and "dry" to the touch.

### Step 5: The Pastry Cream

Boil the milk with the vanilla. In a bowl, whisk the yolks and sugar, then add the cornstarch. Pour some of the hot milk over the mixture, stir, and pour everything back into the saucepan. Thicken over the heat, stirring for 1 minute after boiling. Add the butter, film in contact, and let cool in the fridge.

### Step 6: Filling

Once the puffs and cream are cold, poke a small hole under the puff or cut off the top. Lightly whisk the cream to loosen it and fill the puffs using a piping bag.

---

## 💡 Chef's Tips

- **Craquelin:** For perfectly round and crispy puffs, place a small disc of craquelin (a mixture of butter, brown sugar, and flour) on each puff before baking.
- **Storage:** Fill the puffs only at the last moment to prevent them from softening in contact with the cream.
- **Baking:** If possible, turn off the oven and leave the puffs for 5 minutes with the door slightly ajar at the end of baking to perfect the drying.
