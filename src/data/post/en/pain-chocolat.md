Called **Pain au Chocolat** in most of France and **Chocolatine** in the Southwest, this pastry is a variation of the croissant dough. Its peculiarity lies in its rectangular shape and its two dark chocolate bars that melt delicately during baking.

---

## 🛠️ Technical Sheet

- **Preparation time:** 1.5 hours (ideally over 2 days)
- **Resting time:** 12 hours (Cold)
- **Proofing time:** 2 hours 15 minutes
- **Cooking time:** 15-18 minutes
- **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Expert)
- **Servings:** 10 to 12 pastries

## 🛒 Ingredients

- **Base:** Use the same recipe for "Deterempe" and "Tourage Butter" as for the [Croissants](/en/croissant).
- **Filling:** 20 to 24 chocolate baking sticks (resistant to baking).
- **Glaze:** 1 egg yolk + 1 tbsp milk.

---

## 👨‍🍳 Detailed Method (Step by Step)

### Step 1: Preparation of Leavened Puff Pastry

Prepare the initial dough and perform the layering (1 single fold + 1 double fold) as explained in our croissant guide. Let the dough rest in the cold for at least 1 hour after the last fold.

### Step 2: Rolling Out

Roll out the dough on a lightly floured work surface to obtain a large rectangle about **25 cm wide** and 4 mm thick. Trim the edges with a very sharp knife to release the flaky layers.

### Step 3: Cutting and Filling

Cut strips **8 cm wide** (for 25 cm long).

1. Place a chocolate stick 1 cm from the edge of the strip.
2. Fold the dough over the chocolate.
3. Place the second chocolate stick just after the first fold.
4. Roll the rest of the dough without tightening too much. The "seam" (the end of the dough) must be at the bottom so that the pastry doesn't open.

### Step 4: Final Rise (Proofing)

Place the pain au chocolat on a baking sheet. Let rise in a warm room (**25-27°C**) for about **2 hours 15 minutes**. They should become very light and puffed up. If it's too hot, the butter will escape from the dough.

### Step 5: Baking

Preheat your oven to **190°C**. Gently glaze the top of the pastries with the egg yolk/milk mixture. Be careful not to let the glaze run down the sides, which would "seal" the layers of puff pastry and prevent it from rising. Bake for **15 to 18 minutes**.

---

## 💡 Chef's Tips

- **The Chocolate:** Always use "baking sticks" (bâtons boulangers). Standard chocolate melts too quickly and burns, whereas sticks are designed to stay creamy without running everywhere.
- **The Seam:** Make sure the end of the dough is well-positioned UNDER the pain au chocolat on the baking sheet to prevent it from unrolling like a carpet during proofing.
- **The Freshness:** Pain au chocolat is best enjoyed lukewarm, about 15 minutes after coming out of the oven.
