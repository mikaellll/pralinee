**Milk Bread** (Pain au Lait) is somewhere between white bread and brioche. Rich in milk and butter, its crumb is incomparably fine. It is the ultimate comfort bread, to be snacked on plain or filled with chocolate bars.

---

## 🛠️ Technical Sheet

- **Preparation time:** 30 minutes
- **Resting time:** 2.5 hours
- **Cooking time:** 12-15 minutes
- **Difficulty:** ⭐ (Easy)
- **Servings:** 10 small rolls

## 🛒 Ingredients

- **500g** T45 (or T55) flour
- **250ml** whole milk (lukewarm)
- **60g** granulated sugar
- **10g** salt
- **20g** fresh baker's yeast
- **80g** unsalted butter (at room temperature)
- **1 egg** for glazing
- **Pearl sugar** (optional for decoration)

---

## 👨‍🍳 Detailed Method (Step by Step)

### Step 1: Kneading (The Texture)

In your mixer bowl, pour the flour, sugar, and salt. Crumble the yeast on the other side (salt and yeast should not touch directly at the beginning). Pour in the lukewarm milk. Knead at slow speed for 10 minutes. Then add the diced butter and knead again until the dough is smooth, elastic, and completely pulls away from the sides of the bowl.

### Step 2: Bulk Fermentation

Form a ball, cover with a cloth, and let rest in a warm room for **1.5 hours**. The dough should double in volume.

### Step 3: Shaping (Navettes)

Degas the dough by pressing it with your fist. Divide it into 10 pieces of about 90g. Form balls, then roll them into small elongated cylinders (shuttle shape/navette). Place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

### Step 4: Final Rise

Let rise again for **1 hour**, away from drafts.

### Step 5: Finishing and Baking

Preheat your oven to **180°C**. Glaze the milk breads with the beaten egg. The traditional technique is to make small regular scissor snips on top (ear-of-wheat incisions) to create a nice texture. Bake for **12 to 15 minutes** until they are golden brown.

---

## 💡 Chef's Tips

- **The Softness:** To keep the rolls soft, do not overbake. They should be just golden. When taking them out of the oven, you can brush them with a light sugar syrup to make them shiny.
- **The Snack:** Insert a chocolate bar inside just before eating for a classic children's treat.
- **Storage:** Wrap them in a plastic bag as soon as they are cold so they don't dry out.
